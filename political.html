Abubakar Mohammed's Political Agenda

Education for All

Universal and Compulsory Education is the key to economic development. All children and Youths in our community must be educated. It is moral and political obligation of every politician to ensure all necessary resources at local, state and federal government levels are properly harnessed to achieve the goal of “Education for All”

Full Employment

I believe everyone should be fully and gainfully employed, from our youth to adults. The government alone cannot create jobs for our people and it should not pretend it can. But the government can create sustainable and enabling environment to attract private sector investments, infrastructure developments and entrepreneurship. Katsina Central is blessed with tremendous natural resources and human capital that can be harnessed in order to achieve the goal of “full employment”

Food and Nutrition

No one in our communities should get sick or die because of hunger. Those that have much should be able to help those that have not. A swift intervention programs that will attract ample food supply to our needy people must be a priority. Our children should have access to highly nutritious diet on a regularly basis. Agriculture should be promoted in our communities.

Good Health and Wellbeing

Access to good healthcare facilities. Improvements to our current health facilities requires urgent attention. More facilities should be built and services in small villages and communities. Infant mortality and morbidity rate should be reduced to barest minimum. Collaboration with international aid agencies, department of health and private health institutions should be encouraged and supported.

Security and Law Enforcement

Our law enforcement agencies should be supported by encouraging community participation and cooperation at the local level. There is an urgent need to ensure our communities are safe in order that we may live in peace and unity. Justice and equity should be promoted in our communities. “No one is above the law of the land”
