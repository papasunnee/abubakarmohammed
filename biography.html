MILESTONE CONTRIBUTION TO EMPLOYMENT GENERATION IN NIGERIA

As Deputy Director Planning, Research and Statistics, I designed and developed template for monitoring, evaluation and impact assessment of employment promotion schemes of the directorate.

As State Coordinator, NDE Kaduna state (1988-2003), I contributed in promotion of peace and tranquility in the state through massive engagement of unemployed youths in productive economic activities (counseling services, monitoring skills acquisition and employment activities). This encouraged the NDE corporate headquarters in the setting-up the NDE JOB CENTER.

- Successful establishment of the job centre in all the state of federation
- As Director PR & S, I carried out a nationwide census of unemployed persons. the field date generated from the exercise immensely influenced the design of NDE’s new schemes which were relevant to skills need and promotion of employability for both urban and rural unemployed.

As director, small scale enterprises I adopted and adapted the international labour organization enterpreneurship development training modules relevant to the peculiarities of the federation. This did not only strengthen the entrepreneurship training schemes of directorate but placed on a vintage position to the extent that:

- It provided key material relevant to Bayero university, Kano for the development of its curriculum for entrepreneurship education.
- The NDC became a parter/facilitator for the establishment of central bank of Nigerian entrepreneurship development centres (EDC’S) nationwide.

Also under my leadership the Department employed and strengthen collaborative initiatives with federal government such as the bank of industry, national economic reconstruction fund (NERFUND) etc. this provided NDE skills acquisition training and empowerment beneficiaries with easy access to business financing.

At the time I took over as Director-General NDE(Nov. 2007) job creation activities in the Directorate were at lowest ebb; staff morale was very low due to lack of promotion, poor staff welfare and general working conditions; weak targeting of unemployed persons ; and so on

These have steadily reversed during my 8 year tenure as Director-General the Directorate is Federal Government agency that proactively tackles unemployment. Some of my modest achievements include;

By the end 2007 when I came in as directorate, the directorate whose major tool for generation of mass employment opportunities is skills acquisition training had no skills acquisition center of its own. Within the past 8 years in addition to running our regular programmes/schemes the NDE has;

Acquired skills centers/parks as follows

i. 74 vocation skills acquisition center fully (2 specialized skills centers) equipped with modern tools and equipment for training unemployed persons to become technicians/ artisans. Four of centers have full boarding facilities.
ii. 28 agricultural skills centers.
iii. 10 common facility centres.
iv. 2 agricultural parks

Conducted a PLANNING RETEREAT in 2010 for members of NDE executive management and all 37 coordinators in Abuja.

Conducted 2nd retreat in 2014 with theme REPOSITIONING THE DIRECTORATE IN MEETING UP WITH THE DYNAMICS OF LABOUR MARKET TOWARDS MORE JOD CREATION IN THE FACE OF EMERGING SOCIO-ECONOMIC CHALLENGES’

Provided numerous capacity building and training opportunities both locally and internationally that reshaped the abilities of staff and management of the directorate. such tours include:

i. Study tour for all Directors state coordinators and some programme officers to employment generation programmes and projects in the republic of turkey.
ii. Courses at WEIZ centre for development studies, Rehobot, ISRAEL.
iii. Study tour to Soughai Farms, Porto Novo, Republic of Benin; and so on.

The foregoing leveraged the Directorate to design and implement impactful job creation and empowerment programmes/schemes embedded with quick - wins. Some of these schemes are: The re-engineered Graduate Attachment Programme (GAP) whose success lead to its adoption by SURE-P under the pseudo name Graduate Internship Scheme (GIS); the NDE economic and finance counseling clinic (NDE-efcc), its profound influence lead to endorsement of NDE as a partner for the YOU-WIN Programme implementation; the GRaduate Coaching Scheme (GCS), this allows secondary school leavers to make up their deficiencies towards obtaining the minimum credit passess required by academic instituitions and employers of labour thereby arresting early entry into unemployment under the NEET phenomena (Not in education, Employment or Training). It also provides transient jobs for unemployed holders of bachelor's degree in Education. The Solar Energy Training Scheme SETS). Under this scheme unemployed tertiary institution graduates are trained and equipped with skills in solar energy production (electricity) and maintenance. Preparation, Processing and Packaging Training Scheme (PPPTS) which creates jobs through promotion of value-addition and inclusive growth. Special Micro Empowerment Scheme (SMES) is a social-safety scheme that targets and assists the poorest of the poor, internally Displaced Persons and other vulnerable groups; and so on

NDE’s International Support to Employment

i). In the year 2011 the Directorate assisted the Sierra Leone Government in undertaking Environmental Scan and analysis of the country, highlighted relevant baseline, and recommended programmes for combating mass unemployment.
ii). The Directorate in the year 2012 collaborated with Technical Aid Corps (TAC) and posted Technicians/Artisans to the Republic of Namibia for two year technical aid programme.
At the point of my exit as Director-General, NDE
i). A quick-win intervention project aimed at creating jobs and engaging over 8 million unemployed persons into productive economic activities was presented to the Secretary to the Government of the Federation in October, 2015t.
ii). Preparations were completed for NDE to host the first ever organized National Stakeholders Forum for Employment Creation.
